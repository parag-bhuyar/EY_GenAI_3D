Why Does LangChain Even Exist?
Let's look at the real problem first. Then the answer will make sense.

🔧 The problem — yesterday, in M1.2

You wrote code to do ONE thing. Send a message to the AI. Wait for a reply. Check the reply is correct. Try again if it's broken.

That's already a lot of code, for just one step. A real banking app needs many steps, chained together. Writing every connection by hand gets slow. And easy to break.

🧩 The fix — LangChain

LangChain is a box of ready-made parts. Calling a model. Checking the reply. Retrying. Connecting steps together. All already built, tested, and ready to use.

You just snap the parts together — like LEGO. You don't build each part from scratch anymore.

Concept B
Snap The Pieces Together
In LangChain, every piece is a LEGO block. You snap them together with one symbol: |

🧱 Mental model: every step is a LEGO block

LangChain calls each block a Runnable. A Runnable takes something in, and gives something out — just like a LEGO block snaps onto the next one.

Snap enough Runnables together, and you get a Chain — like parts moving down an assembly line. Each stage does its one job, then passes the result on.

Try it — Build the chain, one block at a time
Click 1 → 2 → 3 → 4, in order. Watch it grow.
extraction_prompt

Stage 1 — the Recipe. Just the prompt: a system message plus a slot for the customer's text.

📝
Stage 1 — Prompt = Recipe
A recipe tells a chef exactly what to make. The prompt tells the model exactly what to do.
👨‍🍳
Stage 2 — Model = Chef
The chef reads the recipe and cooks. The model reads the prompt and replies.
🧾
Stage 3 — Structured Output = Bill Format
A restaurant bill always has the same format: item, price, total. Structured output forces the model's reply into a fixed format too.
📞
Stage 4 — Retry = Call The Customer Again
Didn't hear them clearly? You call back and ask again. Retry does the same thing to the model.
🤔 In extraction_prompt | model, what does the | symbol do?
B is right! It's a lot like a Unix pipe. The prompt's finished message becomes the model's input. Left to right, one stage feeds the next.

The Agent Decides, You Don't
Think of it like GPS choosing your route. You don't pick turn by turn — it decides for you.

🗺️ Mental model: Agent = GPS

A GPS looks at the traffic and picks a route. Nobody hard-coded "if traffic, turn left." It decides, every time, based on what it sees.
