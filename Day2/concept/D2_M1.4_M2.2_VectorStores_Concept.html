Same question. Two very different journeys.

How semantic search decides
Vector = an arrow pointing somewhere. Same direction = same meaning.

This "closeness check" has a real name: cosine similarity. Score of 1.0 = same direction = same meaning. Real embeddings use 1,536 numbers, not 2 — same idea, just bigger.

Query: "How do I get a higher spending limit on my credit card?"
A is right! When the customer's own words match the passage's words, keyword search wins — fast, simple, correct.
Keyword search wins, clearly. "Credit," "limit," "card" — exact matches. No tie, no luck needed.

But semantic search has its own weak spot: exact numbers and codes. "4521" and "4522" look almost the same to it. Real systems often combine both — called hybrid search. You'll hear this again in M2.3.
🧠 Which is the better example of keyword search WINNING?
A is right! Exact words and numbers are keyword search's home turf. Paraphrased meaning is semantic search's job — that's option B, the fraud example from above.
